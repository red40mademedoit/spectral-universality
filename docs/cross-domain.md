# Cross-Domain Analysis

## The Claim

Eigenvalue spacing statistics serve as a universal probe of correlation structure across domains:
- Number theory (Riemann zeros)
- Linguistics (document embeddings)
- Biophysics (protein vibrations, photosynthetic transport)

## What's Established

1. **RMT universality** is a mathematical theorem: large random matrices from the same symmetry class share the same eigenvalue statistics regardless of entry distribution. This is proven for Wigner matrices and extensions.

2. **GUE statistics for Riemann zeros** is proven asymptotically (Montgomery's pair correlation, 1973) and verified numerically to extraordinary precision (Odlyzko, 1987+).

3. **RMT statistics in quantum systems** (nuclear spectra, quantum billiards, etc.) is well-established experimentally and theoretically since Wigner (1950s).

## What We've Shown

1. **Prime counting functions**: Explicit formula verification at 3 scales. Detection of 50/50 zeros with measured amplitudes matching 1/|ρ|. Diagnostic of why gap methods fail.

2. **Embedding spaces**: ⟨r⟩ varies across document corpora (0.47-0.63) and correlates with organizational structure rather than topic. Needs finite-size controls.

3. **Protein vibrations**: Brody q varies across functional classes (0.1-0.67) from large Hessian matrices. Statistically sound (matrices are large).

4. **Photosynthetic Hamiltonians**: Suggestive correlation between Brody q and transport efficiency. Statistically weak (N=7 matrices, small sample).

## What's NOT Established

1. **Cross-domain equivalence**: Finding RMT statistics in multiple systems doesn't prove a deeper connection. Universality PREDICTS this — it's not evidence of a shared mechanism.

2. **"GUE attractor"**: No physical mechanism connecting GUE statistics to optimization across domains.

3. **Causal direction**: Does organizational structure cause GUE statistics, or do both emerge from some third factor (e.g., system complexity)?

## Honest Assessment of Each Domain

| Domain | Statistical strength | Novelty | Publishability |
|--------|---------------------|---------|----------------|
| Primes | Strong (50/50, 3 scales) | Low (verification) | Methods paper |
| Embeddings | Medium (needs γ controls) | Medium | ML venue after controls |
| Proteins | Strong (large matrices) | Medium-High | Biophysics journal |
| Photosynthesis | Weak (N=7, small sample) | High if validated | Needs more data first |

## The Synthesis Paper

A cross-domain review connecting all four would be valuable IF:
- Each individual result stands on its own
- The cross-domain claims are carefully scoped (analogy, not equivalence)
- The methodology section is honest about limitations

Venue: Review article in a physics or complexity journal (e.g., Physics Reports, J. Stat. Mech.).

NOT a Nature paper until the photosynthesis work has larger matrices and more systems.
